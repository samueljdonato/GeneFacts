What are Genes?

Genes are segments of DNA that contain the instructions for making proteins. They are the basic units of heredity and determine many of our traits, from eye color to disease susceptibility.

DNA, RNA, and Proteins

DNA (Deoxyribonucleic Acid): The molecule that stores genetic information in all living organisms.

RNA (Ribonucleic Acid): A molecule that helps translate genetic information from DNA into proteins.

Proteins: Large molecules that perform most functions in cells, from structure to metabolism.

Gene Expression

Gene expression is the process by which information from a gene is used to synthesize functional gene products, usually proteins. This process involves:

  • Transcription: DNA is copied into RNA
  • Translation: RNA is used to make proteins

Chromosomes

Humans have 23 pairs of chromosomes (46 total) in most cells. Each chromosome contains hundreds to thousands of genes. Chromosomes are numbered 1-22, plus the sex chromosomes X and Y.

Entrez Gene Database

Entrez Gene is a database maintained by NCBI that provides information about genes from various organisms. Each gene has a unique Entrez ID number that serves as a stable identifier.

Common gene types include:

  • Protein-coding: Genes that encode proteins
  • Non-coding RNA: Genes that produce functional RNA molecules
  • Pseudogenes: Non-functional gene copies

Why Study Genes?

Understanding genes helps us:

  • Diagnose and treat genetic diseases
  • Develop new medicines
  • Understand evolution and biodiversity
  • Improve crops and livestock
  • Solve crimes through DNA analysis
